## The Structural Case for Gold

Dalio's *"safest money"* in high-debt and devaluation cycles. At the World Governments Summit in February 2026, Dalio reiterated gold's role as the primary store of value when governments inflate away debt. J.P. Morgan projects $6,300/oz by year-end 2026; Goldman Sachs forecasts $5,400.

The structural case — $38 trillion in U.S. debt, sustained central bank buying, and accelerating de-dollarization — remains firmly intact. Gold at 10.5% puts the portfolio inside the 10–15% Dalio target range.

## Central Bank Buying Trend

Central banks around the world have been net buyers of gold for over a decade, with purchases accelerating since 2022. China, India, Poland, and Turkey have been leading accumulators. This structural demand floor supports prices independent of retail investor sentiment.

The de-dollarization trend — countries seeking alternatives to U.S. dollar reserves — creates additional tailwinds for gold as a neutral reserve asset with no counterparty risk.

## Dalio Alignment

Gold serves as the portfolio's primary hedge against currency devaluation. In Dalio's framework, gold is "the one asset that is no one else's liability." During periods of high government debt, monetary expansion, and geopolitical fragmentation, gold historically preserves purchasing power.

The 10–15% target range reflects Dalio's view that gold should be a meaningful but not dominant portfolio component — large enough to provide real protection but not so large that it creates drag during risk-on periods.

## Price Outlook

- **J.P. Morgan:** $6,300/oz by year-end 2026
- **Goldman Sachs:** $5,400/oz target
- **Key drivers:** Persistent central bank buying, geopolitical uncertainty, real rate expectations
- **Risk factors:** Rapid Fed tightening, strong dollar rally, reduced geopolitical tensions
