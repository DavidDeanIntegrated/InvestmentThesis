## Guarding Against Survivorship Bias

International diversification guards against the wealth destruction seen in most countries pre-1945 — the precise survivorship bias Dalio warns about. Using only U.S. historical returns as representative is one of the most common and costly investing mistakes.

## Forward Return Expectations

Vanguard projects 4.9%–6.9% average annual returns for non-U.S. equities over the next decade, compared to lower projections for domestic equities. VXUS provides broad, low-cost access to developed and emerging markets in a single ETF.

The valuation gap between U.S. and international markets has widened significantly, creating a potential mean-reversion opportunity for patient investors.

## Dalio Alignment

Dalio's framework emphasizes that no single country's market should dominate a truly balanced portfolio. The "Big Cycle" applies to all nations — periods of rising and declining empires follow patterns that make geographic diversification essential.

VXUS at 6.1% provides a meaningful but not overweight international allocation, consistent with the portfolio's growth-tilted approach that favors U.S. equities while maintaining protective diversification.
