## Portfolio Growth Engine

The equities sleeve is a balanced growth engine composed of four sub-sleeves: **VTI** (broad US core, ~21%), **VTV** (value tilt, ~8%), **NVDA / TSM / MSFT** (quality compounders, ~15%), and **PLTR / RKLB** (high-conviction, ~7%). The structure avoids relying on any single theme.

## Why 55–60% Equities

The 55–60% equity target is deliberately growth-tilted versus Dalio's more conservative All Weather (30% stocks). This is appropriate for a longer time horizon where the portfolio can weather volatility in exchange for higher long-run compounding.

## Sub-Sleeve Breakdown

### VTI — Broad U.S. Core (~21%)

The anchor of the equities sleeve. Provides diversified exposure to the entire U.S. stock market at near-zero cost (0.03% ER). First to buy during rebalancing, last to sell.

### VTV — Value Tilt (~8%)

Targets mature, dividend-paying companies that tend to outperform during inflationary periods. Complements VTI by adding a tilt toward cash-flow-generative businesses.

### Quality Compounders — NVDA, TSM, MSFT (~15%)

Three companies with structural competitive advantages, massive R&D moats, and long-duration growth runways. Held as a group with a 14–16% target range. Equal-weight rebalancing within the group.

### High Conviction — PLTR, RKLB (~7%)

Asymmetric bets on transformative technology platforms. Treated as "hold-and-dilute" positions — no forced buying during rebalancing. These are small, concentrated bets that Dalio would classify as high-conviction plays with defined risk.

## Rebalancing Approach

- **Portfolio-level:** If total equities drifts outside 55–60%, money must enter or leave the sleeve
- **Internal:** If total is on-target but sub-sleeves are imbalanced, shift within equities
- **Sell priority:** High conviction first, then quality compounders, then broad market last
- **Buy priority:** VTI first, then VXUS if under 6%, then VTV if under 7%
