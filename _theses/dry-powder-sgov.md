## Tactical Dry Powder

SGOV is a high-quality 0–3 month T-bill ETF earning ~4.5–5% while waiting for better equity entry points. SGOV is *tactical* — not a permanent holding. Its role is to earn a safe yield and then deploy at scale when the four drivers (growth, inflation, risk premiums, discount rates) improve for stocks.

## Target Allocation

At 16%, it sits squarely in the 14–17% target range: enough to deploy meaningfully at 10%, 15%, and 25%+ drawdowns without holding so much cash that the real drag becomes a material headwind.

## Deployment Strategy

The SGOV position is governed by a three-tier deployment ladder:

1. **-10% SPY drawdown:** Deploy $180 into VTI (broad U.S. core)
2. **-15% SPY drawdown:** Deploy $180 into quality compounders (NVDA/MSFT/TSM)
3. **-25%+ SPY or VIX >40:** Aggressive deploy ~$247 into VTI + VXUS + conviction names

The permanent floor is **$150** — true emergency cash that is never deployed regardless of market conditions.

## Replenishment Rules

- 100% of new contributions go to SGOV until it reaches 15.7% target
- Opportunistic refill: sell 40% of deployed tranche if SPY recovers +10%
- Priority in the contribution order: SGOV is always #1

## Dalio Alignment

Dalio views cash as a strategic tool, not a wasted asset. Having deployable capital during market stress allows mechanical buy-low execution — the core engine of the rebalancing strategy. The yield on T-bills means the portfolio earns a real return while maintaining this optionality.
