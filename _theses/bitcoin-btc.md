## Modern Hard-Asset Hedge

Bitcoin serves as a modern hard-asset hedge. Combined with GLD (10.5%), the portfolio carries a substantial allocation in "real money" — above Dalio's ideal 10–15% combined target, but acceptable given the BTC position is held at cost and will dilute naturally as new contributions flow to other sleeves.

## Natural Dilution Strategy

No forced rebalance is required. As the portfolio grows toward $8K–$13K+, BTC's percentage weight compresses organically into the 8–12% target range without triggering a taxable event. The strategy is to let time do the rebalancing work here.

This approach is tax-efficient and avoids the psychological difficulty of selling a high-conviction position during a potential multi-year appreciation cycle.

## Dalio Alignment

While Dalio has been cautious about Bitcoin specifically, his framework supports holding assets that serve as alternatives to fiat currency during periods of monetary expansion. Bitcoin's fixed supply of 21 million coins makes it structurally different from any government-issued currency.

The key insight from Dalio's framework: "Diversify well across currencies and across countries, and don't assume that the currency you earn or spend in is also the best one to hold." Bitcoin represents the ultimate alternative currency position.

## Risk Management

- **Special rebalancing rule:** Hold at current weight; let dilution happen naturally
- **Trigger exception:** Rebalance only if >5% absolute drift occurs
- **No mechanical buying:** Never add BTC during regular rebalancing
- **Volatility accepted:** The position size will fluctuate significantly; this is by design
- **Tax efficiency:** Avoiding forced sales preserves long-term capital gains treatment
