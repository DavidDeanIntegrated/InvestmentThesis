## Why Broad Commodities

BCI tracks the Bloomberg Commodity Index Total Return (BCOM) — the broadest, most diversified commodity benchmark, with balanced exposure across energy, metals, agriculture, and livestock. The purest expression of Dalio's "buy any stuff that beats inflation." No K-1, 0.26% expense ratio.

## The Inflation Case

The 2026 inflation case is building: Wellington projects conditions "ripe for an inflationary boom," Invesco expects commodity prices to push CPI higher, and PIMCO recommends "modest, diversified allocations across gold and broad commodities."

Commodities provide returns that are structurally uncorrelated with both stocks and bonds, making them particularly valuable during inflationary periods when traditional financial assets tend to underperform.

## Dalio Alignment

In Dalio's All Weather framework, commodities serve as direct protection against unexpected inflation — one of the four key economic environments the portfolio must survive. Unlike gold (which is primarily a monetary/devaluation hedge), broad commodities respond to real supply-demand dynamics across the global economy.

The 4% allocation complements GLD's 10.5% to create a combined real-assets sleeve of approximately 14.5%, sitting within the 14–16% target range.

## Characteristics

- **Benchmark:** Bloomberg Commodity Index Total Return (BCOM)
- **Expense ratio:** 0.26%
- **Tax efficiency:** No K-1 (unlike many commodity funds)
- **Exposure:** Energy, metals, agriculture, livestock — balanced across sectors
- **Rebalancing role:** Maintain at ~4% with occasional top-ups from new contributions
